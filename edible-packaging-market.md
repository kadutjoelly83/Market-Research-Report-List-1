<p><p>The edible packaging market comprises various types, including polysaccharides, lipids, surfactants, and protein films. Polysaccharide-based films, made from materials like starch and cellulose, offer good barrier properties. Lipid films, derived from fats and oils, enhance moisture resistance and prevent spoilage. Surfactant-based films improve the texture and stability of packaging materials. Protein films, sourced from dairy or plant proteins, provide strong barriers against gases and moisture. Additionally, other emerging materials are being developed to enhance functionality and sustainability.</p></p>
