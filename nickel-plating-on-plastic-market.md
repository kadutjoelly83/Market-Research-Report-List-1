<p><p>The nickel plating on plastic market primarily involves two types: traditional plating and electroless plating. Traditional plating uses electrochemical processes to apply nickel onto a conductive layer, often requiring surface preparation to enhance adhesion. Electroless plating, however, is a chemical process that deposits nickel uniformly without electricity, making it suitable for complex shapes and non-conductive surfaces. Both methods enhance surface properties, providing corrosion resistance, improved durability, and aesthetic appeal, thus finding applications in automotive, electronics, and consumer goods industries.</p></p>
