<p><p>The high purity Silicon Carbide (SiC) powder market comprises two primary types: β-SiC and α-SiC. β-SiC, known for its cubic crystal structure, offers superior thermal stability and suitability for high-frequency applications, making it popular in electronics and advanced ceramics. In contrast, α-SiC, with a hexagonal structure, boasts higher hardness and wear resistance, primarily utilized in abrasives, cutting tools, and semiconductor components. Both types cater to diverse industrial demands, driving growth in sectors like automotive, electronics, and aerospace.</p></p>
