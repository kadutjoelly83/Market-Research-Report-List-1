<p><p>Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) is a thermal analysis technique used to measure heat flows associated with phase transitions in materials as a function of temperature and time. The market is primarily segmented into two types: Heat Flux DSC, which measures the difference in heat flow between a sample and a reference, and Power Compensated DSC, which maintains a constant temperature by adjusting the heat flow to the sample. Both types provide valuable insights into material properties, stability, and thermal behavior.</p></p>
