<p><p>Resistance Temperature Detectors (RTDs) are precision temperature sensors that utilize the predictable change in electrical resistance of materials, typically platinum, with temperature variation. The market features three configurations: Two-wire, Three-wire, and Four-wire. Two-wire configurations are simple but less accurate due to lead resistance influence. Three-wire setups compensate for lead resistance, offering improved accuracy, while Four-wire configurations eliminate lead resistance entirely, providing the highest precision. Each type serves different applications, balancing complexity, cost, and accuracy according to specific needs.</p></p>
